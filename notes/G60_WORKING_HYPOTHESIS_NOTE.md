# G60 Working Hypothesis Note

## Working hypothesis

The clustered mod-2 bridge law now visible on `G15` is not fundamental.
It is the downstairs quotient shadow of a richer chamber-transport law on `G60`, with `G30` as the intermediate signed-sheet layer.

So the current ladder is hypothesized to be:

\[
G60 \longrightarrow G30 \longrightarrow G15
\]

with the following roles.

---

## 1. G60 as transport carrier

`G60` is hypothesized to carry the real local transport mechanism.

This means:
- chamber/flag continuation lives there,
- local closure and exchange behavior live there,
- the distinction between “ordinary square-type continuation” and “twisted continuation” is likely geometric upstairs before it is binary downstairs.

In this reading, `G60` is not merely a larger graph.
It is the first level where the transport law is genuinely present.

---

## 2. G30 as signed-sheet shadow

`G30` is hypothesized to be the first level where the `G60` transport law appears as a binary sheeted object.

So the passage

\[
G60 \to G30
\]

is expected to compress chamber transport into:
- same-sheet continuation,
- cross-sheet continuation.

This is why the cocycle first becomes visible here.

In this reading, the signed lift on `G30` is the first binary shadow of the richer transport structure upstairs.

---

## 3. G15 as parity-class quotient

`G15` is hypothesized to be the quotient where the binary signed-sheet data becomes visible as loop parity classes.

So the passage

\[
G30 \to G15
\]

turns sheet behavior into:
- even loop classes,
- odd loop classes.

That is why `G15` sees:
- even square-type families,
- odd twist-type families,

rather than the full transport mechanism itself.

---

## 4. Interpretation of the current cluster law

The current local invariant law is

\[
R = 0,\qquad Q = A+\sigma,\qquad W = A+\tau,
\]

with
- anchor invariant \(A\),
- square-cluster invariant \(\sigma\),
- twist-cluster invariant \(\tau\).

Working hypothesis:
these are not ultimate primitive quantities.

Rather, they are the quotient images of three upstream transport sectors on `G60`:

- one anchored transport channel,
- one square-side continuation sector,
- one twist-side continuation sector.

So the present cluster law on `G15` is expected to be the mod-2 receipt of a more geometric chamber-transport law on `G60`.

---

## 5. Even/odd family interpretation upstairs

Downstairs on `G15`, the actual-edge scan produced:

- a dominant even 4-cycle family,
- a smaller odd family.

Working hypothesis:
this even/odd split is the quotient shadow of an upstairs distinction on `G60` between:

- closure without sector exchange,
- closure with a sector/Petrie-like exchange defect.

So what appears below as:
- even square family
- odd twist family

may appear above as:
- transport sectors with ordinary closure,
- transport sectors with exchanged closure.

---

## 6. Present strongest conjectural form

Present strongest conjectural form:

> The local class-based bridge on `G15` is the quotient of a chamber-transport law on `G60`, with `G30` as the signed-sheet intermediary, and the current cluster invariants \(A,\sigma,\tau\) are the pushed-down binary remnants of upstream transport sectors.

---

## 7. What would confirm this

The next serious confirmation step would be to identify, on the `G60` side, chamber packets or transport sectors whose descent reproduces:

- the anchor channel corresponding to \(A\),
- the square-support packet corresponding to \(\sigma\),
- the twist-support packet corresponding to \(\tau\).

If those can be exhibited explicitly, then the current local bridge would stop being merely a quotient law and would become the verified shadow of an upstairs transport mechanism.

---
