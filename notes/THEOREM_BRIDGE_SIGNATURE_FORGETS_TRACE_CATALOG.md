# Theorem: The Bridge Signature Forgets the Trace Catalog

## Theorem

The local bridge signature is a many-to-one quotient not only of the invariant cube
\[
(A,\sigma,\tau)\in \mathbb Z_2^3,
\]
but also of the finer local trace-class transport catalogs.

In particular, two distinct realized invariant preimages of the same bridge signature
\[
(R,Q,W)=(0,0,1)
\]
namely
\[
(0,0,1)
\quad\text{and}\quad
(1,1,0),
\]
support different local trace catalogs.

Therefore the bridge signature forgets trace-level transport information.

---

## Proof sketch

### 1. Quotient from the invariant cube

The local bridge signature is given by
\[
(R,Q,W)=(0,\;A+\sigma,\;A+\tau).
\]

Hence both
\[
(0,0,1)
\quad\text{and}\quad
(1,1,0)
\]
map to
\[
(0,0,1).
\]

So the bridge signature is already many-to-one on the invariant cube.

### 2. Distinct trace catalogs at the two preimages

The trace catalog for the baseline state \((0,0,1)\) contains:
- odd \((A,O,O,O)\),
- even \((A,E1,E1,E1)\),
- even \((A,E2,E2,E2)\).

The trace catalog for the alternate preimage \((1,1,0)\) contains:
- odd \((A,O,O,O)\),
- even \((A,E1,E1,E1)\),
- odd \((A,E2,E2,E2)\).

Thus the two states share the same bridge signature but differ at trace level.

### 3. Conclusion

Therefore the bridge signature is a quotient not only of the invariant cube but also of multiple distinct local trace catalogs. ∎

---

## Meaning

The bridge signature records only a coarse receipt of the local transport law.

It does not determine:
- the exact local trace alphabet,
- the full odd/even trace distribution,
- or which anchored even sheet carries odd activation inside a shared quotient class.
