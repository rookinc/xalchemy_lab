# Proposition: Two Primitive Oddness Generators

## Proposition

In the current seeded local patch, the realized odd trace law is best understood as arising from:

- two primitive oddness generators,
- one derived closure residue,
- one mixed cancellation law.

---

## Primitive generator G1: anchored exchange

The anchored odd trace

\[
(A,O,O,O)
\]

is the primitive oddness source at the primary bifurcation hinge.

Interpretation:
this is the local exchange branch issued from the anchor.

---

## Primitive generator G2: distal defect

The distal-defect odd family consists of the realized traces

\[
(D,E1,E1,E2),
\]
\[
(D,E1,E1,M+),
\]
\[
(D,E1,E2,E2).
\]

Interpretation:
oddness is generated by distal defect interaction with the continuation sheets.

This is the second primitive oddness source.

---

## Derived residue R: pure E2 closure

The pure sibling-sheet odd trace

\[
(E2,E2,E2,E2)
\]

appears exactly once in the current local patch and does not propagate broadly across the E2-heavy sector scan.

Interpretation:
this trace is best read as a derived closure residue of the sibling even sheet, not as a third primitive oddness generator.

---

## Mixed cancellation law

The mixed trace

\[
(D,E1,O,M+)
\]

is even.

Interpretation:
explicit odd-branch participation can cancel distal defect oddness in one mixed closure pattern.

So the local oddness law is not freely additive.

---

## Strongest current compression

The exact local odd trace catalog is therefore most economically described by:

1. anchored exchange oddness,
2. distal defect oddness,

together with:

3. one derived pure-E2 closure residue,
4. one mixed cancellation law.

---

## Consequence

The local transport law is now compressed beyond the raw trace catalog.

It is no longer best read as five unrelated odd trace classes.
It is better read as:

- two primitive sources,
- one residue,
- one cancellation mechanism.

This is the tightest current conceptual form of the local oddness law.
