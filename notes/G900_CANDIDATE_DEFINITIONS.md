# G900 candidate definitions

## Goal

List possible meanings of `G900` and choose one to test first.

---

## Candidate A

`G900` is a graph on 900 vertices.

### burden
Need adjacency rule.

### risk
May be too literal and may confuse presentation count with graph count.

---

## Candidate B

`G900` is a dense sampled presentation of one triangular closure law.

### burden
Need sampling rule and identification rule.

### strength
Best fit with the current sketch intuition.

---

## Candidate C

`G900` is a chamber-expansion object above `G60`.

### burden
Need a principled expansion from 60 to 900.

### risk
Could be numerology unless tied to an actual refinement law.

---

## Candidate D

`G900` is not a graph but a pre-graph presentation space.

### burden
Need strict type distinction and descent map into graph form.

### strength
Keeps law and carrier separate.

---

## First test choice

Test Candidate B first:

> `G900` is a dense sampled presentation of one upstream triangular closure law.

Reason:
- fits current visual intuition
- does not force premature graph claims
- leaves room for quotient descent
