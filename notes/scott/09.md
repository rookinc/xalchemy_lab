5. The two directions

To prove the theorem, we need two implications.

Direction A. Retained implies sheet-legal

If a branch is retained, then it must be sheet-legal.

Reason

A retained branch survives inside the closed lifted slot-4 regime. If it were sheet-illegal, then one of two things would happen:

it would force an inconsistent hidden sign transition,

or it would fail to continue lawfully under the retained lifted evolution.


Either way it would not be retained.

So:

\text{retained} \Rightarrow \text{sheet-legal}.

This is the easier direction.


---

Direction B. Sheet-legal implies retained

If a branch is sheet-legal, then it is retained.

Reason

This is stronger and more delicate.

To prove it, one must show that the only real obstruction to remaining in the closed lifted slot-4 family is hidden sheet illegality. In other words:

no extra invisible obstruction exists beyond the sheet law,

any sheet-legal lifted state survives as a lawful slot-4 retained branch.


So:

\text{sheet-legal} \Rightarrow \text{retained}.

This is the more substantial direction.
