# Proposition (Paper Voice): Local Oddness Generators

## Proposition

In the current seeded local patch, the realized odd 4-cycle trace classes are generated by three local oddness mechanisms:

1. **anchored exchange oddness**
   \[
   (A,O,O,O),
   \]

2. **distal defect oddness**
   through the realized trace family
   \[
   (D,E1,E1,E2),\quad
   (D,E1,E1,M+),\quad
   (D,E1,E2,E2),
   \]

3. **pure sibling-sheet oddness**
   \[
   (E2,E2,E2,E2).
   \]

Moreover, these generators are not freely additive: the mixed trace
\[
(D,E1,O,M+)
\]
is even, exhibiting a local cancellation law.

---

## Meaning

The exact local odd trace catalog is not most naturally read as five unrelated odd classes.

It is better understood as the image of three primitive oddness generators together with a mixed cancellation principle.

---

## Proof sketch

The trace-class catalog shows that the only realized odd classes are:

- (A,O,O,O)
- (D,E1,E1,E2)
- (D,E1,E1,M+)
- (D,E1,E2,E2)
- (E2,E2,E2,E2)

These separate naturally into:

- one anchor-driven odd class,
- three distal-defect odd classes,
- one pure E2 odd closure class.

The refined transport probe further shows that
\[
(D,E1,O,M+)
\]
is even, so the distal-defect mechanism is not activated in every mixed trace containing \(D\). Hence the local oddness law necessarily includes cancellation.

This establishes the generator-level reading. ∎
