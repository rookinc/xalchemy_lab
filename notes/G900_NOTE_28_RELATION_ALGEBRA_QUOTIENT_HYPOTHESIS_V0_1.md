# G900 Note 28 — Relation-Algebra Quotient Hypothesis v0.1

## Hypothesis
The weighted triangular prism is not a direct quotient of cell adjacency.
It is a quotient of the chiral cross-bit edge-relation algebra.

## Statement
The meaningful upstream object is not the raw cell graph itself, but the classified family of cross-bit carrier relations:
- same-macro
- live directed shift
- absent opposite shift

The weighted prism is an organized export shadow of those relation roles.

## Current evidence
Already established:
- raw carrier adjacency is entirely cross-bit
- naive cell-label quotient maps fail
- simple macro rules fail
- the edge algebra splits into:
  - same_macro = 900
  - live_shift = 405
  - opposite shift = 0

This strongly suggests the prism is relation-level, not cell-level.

## Predictions
If this hypothesis is right, then:
- any successful derivation of the prism must pass through relation families
- face/rung roles should emerge from edge-relation classes, not isolated cell labels
- the chirality of the carrier should survive into the signed normalization story

## Falsifiers
This hypothesis weakens or fails if:
- a direct cell-level quotient recovers the prism cleanly
- relation-family structure turns out to be unnecessary
- the exported prism support can be derived without using the chiral edge algebra

## Honest status
This is broader than the pre-midpoint shell hypothesis.
It is also currently less sharp, but strongly supported.
