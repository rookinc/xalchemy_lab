# G60 Signature Lift Target

## Goal

Define what it would mean to lift the toy framed signature `(H,S)` into the G60 setting.

## Toy source objects

In the toy layer:

- `H` = parity of chart-coordinate flips along the local framed trace
- `S` = signed chart displacement along the local framed trace

Together:

- `(H,S)` = framed transport signature

## Lift question

In G60, what are the corresponding objects?

### Candidate lift of `H`
A G60 analogue of `H` should measure:

- parity of local framed reversals
- or parity of local chart-side flips
- or a local cocycle class on short oriented continuations

### Candidate lift of `S`
A G60 analogue of `S` should measure:

- signed framed displacement
- or oriented continuation bias
- or net chart-side accumulation along a short local path

## Minimal success condition

A successful first lift does not require the full theory.

It only requires:

- a local G60 path
- a chart-relative readout on that path
- a computable parity-like quantity
- a computable displacement-like quantity

## First receipt target

The first G60 lift should produce, on a minimal local patch:

- local framed trace
- candidate `H_G60`
- candidate `S_G60`
- comparison under short path concatenation

## Strongest sentence

The goal is not to force the toy signature onto G60 verbatim, but to identify the smallest G60 parity-like and displacement-like framed quantities that play the same structural role.
