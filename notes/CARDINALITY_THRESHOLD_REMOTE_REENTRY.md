# Cardinality Threshold in Remote Re-entry

This note records the present result of the remote re-entry threshold tests in the 4-hub patch toy.

It is a working interpretation note.
It is not a physics claim.
It is a status marker for what the toy now observably does.

## Main result

Remote hub behavior now appears to obey a clear cardinality threshold:

- three-body arrival is closure-capable
- two-body arrival is transfer-capable but closure-incomplete
- one-body arrival is transport-only and can damp away

Compactly:

**Three closes. Two transfers. One drifts.**

---

## 1. Three-of-three arrival

In the full stressed re-entry case, all three turtles reach the remote signed hub together.

Observed behavior:
- `LLR @ u1R`
- `ABC+_tension`
- sign inversion occurs
- carried stress increases
- mismatch rises
- stored tension appears
- remote deposited stress is recorded

Interpretation:

The full triad is a closure-capable object.

---

## 2. Two-of-three arrival

In the peeled remnant case, one turtle is diverted before remote re-entry and only two turtles arrive together.

Observed behavior:
- `LR @ u1R`
- `sign_transfer+`
- deposited stress occurs
- mismatch rises
- no `LLR`
- no `ABC+_tension`
- no stored tension at the remote hub

Interpretation:

A dyadic remnant remains dynamically active and can still interact with the hub, but it no longer satisfies the closure threshold.

It is transfer-capable, but closure-incomplete.

---

## 3. One-of-three arrival

In the singleton case, only one stressed turtle reaches the remote hub.

Observed behavior:
- no collision at `u1R`
- no sign-transfer event
- no deposited stress
- no mismatch increase
- no closure event
- carried stress damps away during solitary transport

Interpretation:

A singleton is not hub-activating in the present toy.
It is only a transported carrier, and under free travel it can lose its stress before any further structured interaction occurs.

---

## 4. Present law

The cleanest current law is:

**Remote signed hub activation is cardinality-sensitive.**

Broken out explicitly:

- triads activate closure
- dyads activate transfer only
- singletons activate nothing

Equivalent slogan:

**Three closes. Two transfers. One drifts.**

---

## 5. Why this matters

This result sharpens the role of the mediator bundle.

The mediator does not merely hold arbitrary traffic.
It preserves a packet whose later behavior depends on how much of that packet survives to the next signed hub.

So the bundle has an observable threshold structure:

- full bundle -> closure-capable
- partial bundle -> degraded but still lawful
- singleton residue -> non-closing carrier

That is a meaningful distinction.

---

## 6. Current interpretation

The present best interpretation is:

**Closure is not just about stress content.  
Closure is also about arrival multiplicity.**

Imported stress by itself is not enough.

A remote hub can receive:
- a full triad and close,
- a dyad and only transfer,
- or a singleton and do nothing at all.

So the toy now supports the idea that hub law depends on both:
- sign/stress state
- and carrier cardinality

---

## 7. Bottom line

The 4-hub patch now supports a threshold picture of remote re-entry:

- full triads retension
- dyads transfer without closure
- singletons simply travel and damp

That is the current law of the remote re-entry tests.
