## Working LLR assignment
Use LLR as a 3-role grammar, not a literal turn sequence.

Candidate roles:
- L1 = live directed shift
- L2 = same-macro continuation / hold
- R  = centered return / closure renormalization

## Why this assignment
Current carrier facts:
- one live directed shift family exists
- same_macro family is dominant
- opposite directed shift family is absent
- centered signed export law appears only after midpoint/pre-midpoint renormalization

So the most plausible reading is:
- two upstream continuation roles
- one downstream closure role

## Prediction
If this is right, then the G900 mechanism is not a symmetric 3-cycle.
It is a chiral triadic grammar whose third role is closure, not a second live shift.

## Break conditions
This assignment weakens if:
- a second live shift family appears in a principled derivation
- closure can be derived without midpoint/pre-midpoint renormalization
- same_macro turns out not to play a continuation-like role
