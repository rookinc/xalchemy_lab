# Delayed Triad Assembly Note

This note records the present result of the delayed-third-arrival test in the 4-hub patch toy.

## Main result

A triadic closure at a signed hub does not require strict simultaneous arrival.

It is enough that the three-body configuration becomes assembled at the hub after staged prior interactions.

Compactly:

**Triads need not arrive together; they need to assemble.**

---

## Observed result

In the delayed-third test:

- a pair first interacted at `u1R` via repeated `LR -> sign_transfer+`
- the third turtle arrived later
- when all three were present together at `u1R`, the toy produced:

- `LLR @ u1R`
- `ABC+_tension`

with:
- sign inversion to `+,+,+`
- stress increase to `1,1,1`
- mismatch increase
- stored tension increase at `u1R`

So delayed assembly still closes.

---

## Interpretation

This means the hub is not merely an instantaneous coincidence detector.

It can act as a stateful staging site:
- partial arrivals can occur first
- transfer interactions can happen first
- later completion of the triad can still trigger closure

So the present law is:

**Closure requires triadic co-presence, but not strict simultaneous first arrival.**

---

## Current ladder

- one present -> inert or carrier-only
- two present -> transfer-capable
- three present -> closure-capable

with the important refinement:

- the three need not arrive on the same first-contact tick
- they only need to become assembled at the hub

---

## Bottom line

The 4-hub patch now supports staged hub assembly.

Closure is not strictly about simultaneous arrival.
It is about eventual triadic occupancy of the signed hub.
