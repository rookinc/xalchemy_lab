# G60 15-Anchor Cube Hypothesis

## Hypothesis

The correct first G60-facing framed patch is not an arbitrary chamber-centered local neighborhood.

It is a cube-based anchored support skeleton with:

- 8 corner anchors
- 6 face-center anchors
- 1 cube-center anchor

for a total of 15 anchored vertices.

## Interpretation

These 15 anchors form the rigid structural support set.

- corners provide extremal boundary anchors
- face centers provide local sheet / face mediators
- the cube center provides the central coupling / reference anchor

The remaining vertices are allowed to vary or deform relative to this rigid support skeleton.

## Strongest sentence

The 15-anchor cube is the rigid framed support set; everything else may wiggle around it.

## Why this matters

This gives a concrete first candidate for the G60 local framed carrier system.

Instead of asking for one arbitrary local patch, we can ask:

- how is framed transport read relative to this anchored cube skeleton?
- what are the local continuation classes at an anchored site?
- how do chart-relative readouts depend on the anchor type?
- what short return paths exist through the anchored support set?
