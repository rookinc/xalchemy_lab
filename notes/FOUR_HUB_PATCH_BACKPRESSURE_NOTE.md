# Four-Hub Patch Note
## Backpressure bundles at mediators, polarity inversion at hubs

This note records the present status of the 4-hub patch experiments in `xalchemy_lab`.

It is a working interpretation note.
It is not a physics claim.
It is a status marker for what the toy is now observably doing.

The main result is now clear enough to state plainly:

**Signed hubs invert and load. Neutral mediators bundle and preserve under back pressure.**

That is the present law of the patch.

---

# 1. Patch structure

The current local 4-hub patch contains:

- positive hubs:
  - `u1L`
  - `u1R`

- negative hubs:
  - `d1L`
  - `d1R`

- neutral mediators:
  - `mL`
  - `mR`

- outer feeders:
  - `u3L`, `u2L`, `u3R`, `u2R`
  - `d3L`, `d2L`, `d3R`, `d2R`

The patch therefore has three clear role classes:

1. **signed hubs**
2. **neutral relay mediators**
3. **outer feeder nodes**

This role distinction now appears to be operationally real, not just a naming convenience.

---

# 2. What the signed hubs do

The signed hubs are the places where the important transformations occur.

At signed hubs, the toy now shows all of the following:

- sign stamping
- mismatch accumulation
- tensioned closure
- stress loading
- stored tension
- stress energy increase
- deposited stress intake from incoming stressed turtles

These are the active sites of inversion and loading.

## Observed hub behavior

### Positive hubs
- `u1L`, `u1R`

These support:
- `B+`
- `sign_transfer+`
- `ABC+_closed`
- `ABC+_tension`

### Negative hubs
- `d1L`, `d1R`

These support:
- `B-`
- `sign_transfer-`
- `ABC-_closed`
- `ABC-_tension`

The positive and negative hub families now behave as clear sign-reversed analogues.

That mirror symmetry has survived enlargement of the carrier.

---

# 3. What the neutral mediators do

The mediators are:

- `mL`
- `mR`

They were initially expected to be passive relay space.

The experiments now support a more precise statement:

They are not passive in the trivial sense, because they can host multi-turtle bundle events.

But they are passive in the following very important operational sense:

- they do **not** invert sign
- they do **not** raise mismatch
- they do **not** increase carried stress
- they do **not** produce hub-ledger loading
- they do **not** create stored tension

So they are not dead wires, but neither are they active flip/load hubs.

Their current best interpretation is:

**neutral compression/relay space under load**

That is exactly why the new label `backpressure_bundle` is appropriate.

---

# 4. Key experimental progression

The present picture emerged in stages.

## Stage A: local left and right channels
The patch first showed that the original local machinery survives enlargement:

- positive support could form at an upper hub
- transport could pass through a mediator
- mismatch could arise at a lower hub
- tension closure could occur there

This worked on the left side and on the right-side mirror.

So the basic local law survived patch enlargement.

## Stage B: lateral crossover
Next, the packet was sent across the patch:

- `u1L -> mL -> mR -> d1R`

This showed that the mid-layer is not merely local relay.
It is a real crossover band.

A packet formed at one signed hub can survive transport through both mediators and still tension-close at a laterally displaced signed hub.

This was a major result.

## Stage C: cross-patch stress transport
Finally, the stressed packet generated at `d1R` was sent onward toward `u1R`.

This produced the most important current result:

- stress generated at one signed hub can be transported across the patch
- the transport passes through a mediator bundle state
- then a remote signed hub can inherit that stress and tension-close itself

That is where the present law comes from.

---

# 5. The decisive trace

The strongest recent experiment is the cross-patch stress transport run.

The important event sequence is:

1. `LL @ u1L -> B+`
2. packet propagates through `mL`
3. packet crosses to `mR`
4. `LR @ d1R -> sign_transfer-`
5. `LLR @ d1R -> ABC-_tension`
6. stressed turtles leave `d1R`
7. all three re-meet at `mR`
8. stressed traffic reaches `u1R`
9. `LLR @ u1R -> ABC+_tension`

This sequence matters because it distinguishes three different event types:

- **formation**
- **bundle preservation**
- **remote reinjection / retensioning**

That is much richer than the original two-hub toy.

# 6. What happens at the first tension site

At `d1R`, the trace shows the first true inversion/load event.

The important line is the `LLR` tension event there:

- incoming sign:
  - `+,+,+`

- outgoing sign:
  - `-,-,-`

- incoming stress:
  - `0,0,0`

- outgoing stress:
  - `1,1,1`

- mismatch rises

So at `d1R` the toy does three things at once:

1. flips sign
2. injects carried stress
3. increases mismatch burden

This is the prototype of a true active hub event.

It is exactly the kind of thing mediators do **not** do.

---

# 7. What happens at the mediator bundle

The critical mediator event is now the one at `mR`, relabeled as:

- `backpressure_bundle`

This was the correct relabeling.

The trace shows:

- incoming sign:
  - `-,-,-`

- outgoing sign:
  - `-,-,-`

- incoming stress:
  - `1,1,1`

- outgoing stress:
  - `1,1,1`

- incoming mismatch:
  - unchanged
- outgoing mismatch:
  - unchanged

That means the mediator bundle does **not** perform inversion.

It does **not** amplify stress.

It does **not** create additional mismatch.

It does **not** locally load a hub ledger.

So what is it doing?

It is holding the stressed, sign-coherent packet together while it remains under pressure in relay space.

That is why “backpressure bundle” is the right phrase.

The mediator is not a flip site.
It is a **pressure-maintaining bundle site**.

This means `u1R` is not merely receiving a packet.

So the second hub is doing real work:

1. it accepts transported stressed traffic
2. it records deposited stress
3. it flips sign again
4. it increases stress loading again
5. it closes under tension

That is a genuine second-stage tension event.

This is no longer just local toy behavior.
It is now bona fide patch-scale propagation behavior.

# 12. Why the mediator event should stay

It would have been easy to delete the mediator triple event and dismiss it as noise.

That would have been premature.

The diagnostic trace shows that the mediator event is structurally meaningful even though it is not an inversion event.

Why it matters:

- it is where the three stressed turtles re-cohere
- it preserves sign and stress without mutation
- it appears between the first and second tension sites
- it marks the bundle as an object in transit rather than just three unrelated walkers

So the mediator triple event should remain in the model.

But it should be named honestly.

That is exactly what `backpressure_bundle` now does.

So the updated interpretation is:

**back pressure creates a coherent relay bundle at the mediator, while polarity/chirality inversion remains hub-local.**

That is stronger than a vague flip suspicion, because it is tied directly to the trace.

# 16. Most likely next experiment

The next sensible experiment is not to add more vocabulary immediately.

It is to test whether mediator bundles can:
- split
- partially damp
- or arrive asymmetrically

In other words:

can a backpressure bundle lose coherence before the next hub, or is it currently too stable?

Good next tests would be:

- separate one turtle from the mediator bundle before re-entry
- force only two turtles to reach the next hub
- insert a free-flight interval after a bundle
- compare deposited stress with and without mediator bundling

That will tell us whether `backpressure_bundle` is:
- a robust transport object
- or only a transient crowded state
