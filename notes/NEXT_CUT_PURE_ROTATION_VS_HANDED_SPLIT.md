# Next Cut: Pure Rotation vs Handed Split

## Goal

Decide whether the four observed states are:

- pure rotations of one motif

or

- a product structure with both rotation and handedness

## Competing models

### Model A: pure rotation
The four states are:

- `s0`
- `s1`
- `s2`
- `s3`

with a single cyclic transition rule.

Interpretation:
Only orientation changes.
Handedness does not create additional distinct states.

### Model B: two-by-two split
The observed family is better understood as:

- two rotation classes
- times two handed classes

So the four states would be:

- `(r0, hL)`
- `(r0, hR)`
- `(r1, hL)`
- `(r1, hR)`

Interpretation:
The apparent four-state family is not just cyclic rotation.
It also includes a binary handed or chart-side distinction.

## What to inspect

To decide between A and B, inspect whether each successive state is obtainable by rotation alone.

Signs that Model B may be needed:

- one state looks like a mirror rather than a rotation
- the inner spiral/core switches side relative to the diagonal
- the anchored contact keeps position while the internal motif changes chirality

## Strongest sentence

The next rigorous question is whether the four-state family is one cyclic rotation orbit or a smaller rotation orbit doubled by handedness.
