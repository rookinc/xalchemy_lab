# Cube Spark Phi^3 / x^2 Cycle

## Cycle

A cube catches a spark.

The spark radiates at `phi^3` and finds the direct path to center.

It then drains to center by `x^2`.

Once the center receives and resolves it, the center radiates back out at `phi^3`.

## Phase interpretation

### Phi^3
Used for:

- ignition
- attractor reach
- outward re-radiation

### x^2
Used for:

- inward drain
- centerward collapse
- core resolution

## Strongest sentence

The cube catches a spark, the spark seeks center at `phi^3`, drains into center by `x^2`, and the center re-radiates the resolved state outward again at `phi^3`.

## Interpretation

This is a breathing boundary-core-boundary cycle with asymmetric scales:

- `phi^3` for triadic field excitation
- `x^2` for inward settling into the core
