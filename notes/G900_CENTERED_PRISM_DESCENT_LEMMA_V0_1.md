## Status
Lemma candidate grounded in the current extracted weighted prism artifact and the checked G900 descent bundle.

## Statement
Let the first exact quotient of the G900 carrier be the weighted triangular prism with extracted edge law:

- top-face edges = 140
- macro-rung edges = 145
- bottom-face edges = 150

Then:

1. the rung weight is the midpoint of the two face weights,
2. the prism law is a symmetric ±5 split around the center 145,
3. and, if the prism-to-triangle collapse identifies top and bottom face edges without adding side-face scalar mass, each descended triangle edge class has base candidate 290.

Equationally:

- 145 = (140 + 150) / 2
- 140 = 145 - 5
- 150 = 145 + 5
- 290 = 140 + 150
- 290 = 2 * 145

## Interpretation
The extracted weighted prism is not just doubled support. It is a *centered* doubled support.

Its three edge strata form a balanced arithmetic spine:

- upper face layer: center minus 5
- rung layer: center
- lower face layer: center plus 5

So the prism is organized around a real midpoint law, not merely around three unrelated weights.

Under the current collapse picture, the top and bottom face layers recombine into the descended triangle edge classes. This produces a uniform base edge candidate:

- AB base = 140 + 150 = 290
- BC base = 140 + 150 = 290
- CA base = 140 + 150 = 290

This already matches the current macro contact count:

- macro contact = 290 = 2 * 145

Under that reading, the descended triangle edge law is governed by face recombination:

- triangle edge base = 290

This closes the numbers into one small lawful chain:

- extracted prism weights: 140, 145, 150
