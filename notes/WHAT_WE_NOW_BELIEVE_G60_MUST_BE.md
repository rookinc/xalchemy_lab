# What We Now Believe G60 Must Be

## Status

This note is a checkpoint in plain language.

It does **not** claim that `G60` has been derived globally.
It records what the current local law now forces us to believe an upstairs `G60` mechanism must look like, if it is to remain compatible with the measured local cube.

---

## 1. The local bridge law is richer than the bridge signature

The bridge signature
\[
(R,Q,W)=(0,\;A+\sigma,\;A+\tau)
\]
is real, but it is only the coarsest receipt.

It forgets:
- invariant-level distinctions,
- trace-catalog distinctions,
- and even anchored sheet-polarity distinctions inside a shared quotient class.

So the bridge signature is not the law itself.

The law lives higher up, at the trace-class transport level.

---

## 2. The local trace law has a normal form

The full currently realized local cube splits into:

1. an anchored control surface,
2. a rigid odd distal backbone,
3. a rigid even distal backbone.

So the local law is not uniform.
It has:
- a small flexible anchored region,
- and a much larger parity-stable distal skeleton.

This is now one of the clearest structural facts on the table.

---

## 3. The distal backbone is rigid on the full realized cube

Across all currently realized local invariant states, the following remain odd:

- `(D,E1,E1,E2)`
- `(D,E1,E1,M+)`
- `(D,E1,E2,E2)`
- `(E2,E2,E2,E2)`

And the following remain even:

- `(D,E1,O,M+)`
- `(E1,E1,M+,M+)`
- `(E1,E2,E2,E2)`
- `(E1,E2,M+,M+)`
- `(E1,M+,M+,O)`
- `(E2,E2,E2,O)`

So the distal part of the law is currently behaving like a rigid transport skeleton.

That is no longer guesswork.
It is measured.

---

## 4. The anchored law is charted

The anchored control grammar does **not** behave like three symmetric abstract bits.

Instead:

- `A` selects the anchored chart,
- `sigma` controls `E1`-sheet activation,
- `tau` controls odd-branch activation,

subject to which anchored chart `A` has selected.

This is the key upgrade.

### A = 0 chart
- `(A,O,O,O)` tracks `tau`
- `(A,E1,E1,E1)` tracks `sigma`
- `(A,E2,E2,E2)` stays even

### A = 1 chart
- `(A,E2,E2,E2)` is always odd
- `(A,E1,E1,E1)` depends on `(sigma,tau)`
- `(A,O,O,O)` depends on `(sigma,tau)`

So `A` is **not** just a swap bit.
It is best understood as a **chart selector** or **anchored phase selector**.

---

## 5. The best current upstairs candidate is a two-phase anchored controller over a rigid distal backbone

The cleanest local candidate mechanism is now:

- one anchored controller with two phases (`A=0` and `A=1`)
- one odd exchange branch
- two sibling continuation sheets
- one rigid distal skeleton

This is the content of Toy G60 Automaton v2.

And the important point is:

**it is not just evocative. it matches the full realized local cube exactly.**

Validation result:
- total checks = 120
- mismatches = 0
- matches = 120

So we now have an explicit local candidate upstairs mechanism that reproduces all currently realized local trace behavior.

---

## 6. What this means for G60

If `G60` is the correct upstairs carrier, then it probably cannot be “just a graph” in the naive sense.

It must at least support:

1. a local anchor packet or chamber packet,
2. an odd exchange branch,
3. two sibling continuation sheets,
4. a rigid distal transport skeleton,
5. a two-chart anchored control grammar.

In other words, the minimal viable `G60` picture is already packet-like and phase-like.

It is not merely combinatorial parity on edges.

## 7. What is evidence, and what is conjecture

### Evidence now in hand
- the exact local trace law
- the local normal form
- the rigid distal backbone on the full realized cube
- the chart split in the anchored grammar
- Toy G60 Automaton v2 reproducing the full realized local cube exactly

### Still conjectural
- that this local automaton is the true descent of an actual global `G60` geometry
- that the two anchored charts arise from a deeper geometric principle rather than a fitted rule
- that the same packet-and-skeleton mechanism extends consistently beyond the currently realized local cube
- that the full upstairs object is truly `G60` rather than some other local cover or packet carrier

So we are no longer at pure speculation, but we are also not yet at global derivation.

---

## 8. Where it could still break

This picture could still fail in at least four ways.

### 1. Beyond the realized local cube
We have matched the full **realized** local cube, but not an arbitrarily enlarged local state space.

### 2. Beyond the current patch
The rigid distal backbone may stop being rigid in a larger or differently seeded patch.

### 3. Under true geometric lifting
The chart split may fail to arise naturally from a genuine `G60` packet geometry and instead be an artifact of the current fitted toy automaton.

### 4. At the global level
Different local packets might fail to glue consistently into a single upstairs global transport law.

So the current result is strong, but still local.
