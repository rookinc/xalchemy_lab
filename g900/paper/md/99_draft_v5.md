# Bounded Slot-4 Exclusion at the Frame-2 Seam in the G900 Witness Machine

## Abstract

We study a localized obstruction phenomenon in the normalized G900 witness machine. Under the baseline two-step policy, the machine is monotone in normalized action distance on the sampled run but produces no nontrivial exact closure, exhibiting contraction without closure. An exact-preference repair resolves the dominant reachable chamber-level failure, showing that much of the original obstruction was due to policy selection rather than deep global impossibility.

After this repair, the residual basin funnels into the frame-2 near-action seam, where the remaining mismatch localizes to normalized slot 4. Exact frame-2 closure requires slot 4 to take the value \(t2\). Starting from a distinguished locked subset of frame-2-nearest witnesses, we study the bounded retained substitution regime generated by single-symbol substitutions from a fixed vocabulary, followed by dihedral normalization and retention by action-distance and exactness filters.

The bounded retained dynamics exclude \(t2\) at slot 4. More sharply, the residual obstruction lies in a distinguished punctured slice of the full normalized frame-2 Hamming-1 seam around the exact prototype
\[
E_2=[o4,s0,t0,s2,t2,s4].
\]
This punctured slice is exactly the one-coordinate deformation family
\[
[o4,s0,t0,s2,x,s4],
\qquad
x\in\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}.
\]

Thus the obstruction is not merely a failed search for closure. It is a rigid local seam law: the full seam decomposes into coordinate slices around the exact prototype, the locked witness regime selects the unique scaffold-preserving punctured slice, and the exact installation value is missing from that slice. The paper makes a bounded theorem claim, not a global impossibility claim, and isolates the remaining structural problem as the direct derivation of this slice-selection law from the substitution, normalization, and classification geometry.

## 1. Introduction

This paper studies a localized obstruction phenomenon in the normalized G900 witness machine.

The starting observation is contraction without closure. Under the baseline two-step policy, the machine is monotone in normalized action distance on the sampled run, yet it produces no nontrivial exact closure. Taken alone, however, this does not yet support a structural theorem. A large part of such a failure could still be due to poor policy selection rather than a genuine geometric obstruction.

That ambiguity is resolved by an exact-preference repair. Computationally, the repair restores the dominant reachable frame-0 chamber, showing that a substantial part of the original failure was contingent rather than intrinsic. What remains after repair is therefore mathematically sharper.

The residual failure localizes to the frame-2 near-action seam and condenses to a single normalized coordinate, slot 4. Exact frame-2 closure requires this slot to take the value \(t2\). The problem is therefore no longer a diffuse global reachability question. It becomes a local installation problem on a distinguished seam.

The present paper sharpens that local problem in two stages. First, it proves a bounded exclusion theorem: in the bounded retained substitution regime generated from a distinguished locked witness set, the exact slot value \(t2\) never appears and no exact frame-2 closure occurs. Second, it identifies the local seam geometry supporting that exclusion. The full normalized frame-2 seam is a Hamming-1 shell around the exact prototype
\[
E_2=[o4,s0,t0,s2,t2,s4],
\]
and the locked witness regime lives in a distinguished punctured slice of that shell. That slice is exactly the one-coordinate deformation family
\[
[o4,s0,t0,s2,x,s4].
\]

This is the paper’s main claim. After chamber-level repair, the residual frame-2 obstruction lies in a distinguished punctured slice of the full normalized Hamming shell, and that slice is exactly the one-coordinate deformation family of the exact prototype.

The result is bounded. The paper does not claim a global impossibility theorem for exact frame-2 closure. Instead, it identifies the precise local geometry of the observed obstruction and thereby reduces the remaining explanation problem to a sharper form: why do the locked witness dynamics preserve this unique scaffold-preserving punctured slice rather than mixing into the other seam directions?

We write

\pi_j(c)=x_j

Let
\[
E_2=[o4,s0,t0,s2,t2,s4]

Let
\[
F_2^{\mathrm{exact}}\subseteq X

## 3. Computational Ladder

The baseline two-step policy is monotone in normalized action distance on the sampled run but produces no nontrivial exact closure. This is the initial contraction-without-closure phenomenon.

Exact-preference repair then recovers the dominant reachable frame-0 chamber. This shows that a substantial component of the earlier failure was due to policy selection rather than an intrinsic obstruction of the witness machine.

After repair, the residual basin funnels into the frame-2 near-action seam. In the distinguished locked witnesses, the remaining mismatch localizes at slot 4. Each locked witness is frame-2-nearest, has no exact one-edit child, and nevertheless remains locally mobile under one-edit perturbation.

The bounded retained regime generated from these locked witnesses saturates computationally by depth 6, with no new states appearing at depths 7 and 8. The total number of visited states is 58,624. No state in this bounded regime is exact frame 2. More strongly, no state in this bounded regime has normalized slot-4 value \(t2\).

These facts motivate the local seam theory developed in the next sections.

## 4. Bounded Exclusion Theorem

### Theorem 4.1. Bounded slot-4 exclusion theorem
Let \(B\) be the bounded retained substitution regime generated from the locked frame-2 witnesses under single-symbol substitution from the fixed vocabulary, dihedral normalization, and retention by the bounded action-distance and exactness filters. Then every retained state \(c\in B\) satisfies
\[
\pi_4(c)\in\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}.
\]
In particular,
\[
\pi_4(c)\neq t2.
\]

### Corollary 4.2. Bounded no exact frame-2 closure
If exact frame-2 closure requires \(\pi_4(c)=t2\), then
\[
B\cap F_2^{\mathrm{exact}}=\varnothing.
\]

## 5. The Full Frame-2 Seam as a Normalized Hamming-1 Shell

### 5.1. Exact normalized frame-2 prototype

The raw frame-2 action cell is
\[
[o4,s4,t2,s2,t0,s0].
\]
Dihedral lexicographic normalization sends this to
\[
E_2=[o4,s0,t0,s2,t2,s4].
\]

### 5.2. Normalized action distance as Hamming distance

By construction, the action-distance used in the classifier is Hamming distance between normalized representatives. Thus the frame-2 seam is naturally a normalized Hamming-1 shell around \(E_2\), restricted to action-cell states with frame 2 among nearest action frames.

\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{full}}=\bigsqcup_{j=0}^5 \Sigma_2^{(j)}.

## 6. The Distinguished Punctured Slot-4 Slice

The full seam is larger than the family selected by the locked witnesses. The relevant local object is a distinguished slice of that full shell.

### 6.1. Puncture identifies the slot-4 slice

Inside the full seam,
\[
\Sigma_2^{(4)}
=
\{c\in\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{full}}:\ \pi_4(c)\neq t2\}.
\]

Indeed, every slice \(\Sigma_2^{(j)}\) with \(j\neq 4\) agrees with \(E_2\) at coordinate \(4\), hence satisfies \(\pi_4=t2\). The slot-4 slice is the unique slice in which the exact prototype value at slot 4 is punctured.

### 6.2. Unique scaffold-preserving slice

The same slice is uniquely characterized by preservation of the normalized scaffold
\[
[o4,s0,t0,s2,\_,s4].
\]
Equivalently,
\[
\Sigma_2^{(4)}
=
\{c\in\Sigma_2^{\mathrm{full}}:\ c_j=(E_2)_j \text{ for } j\in\{0,1,2,3,5\}\}.
\]

So the distinguished slot-4 slice is simultaneously:
- the punctured slice,
- the scaffold-preserving slice,
- the slice later seen as the \(T\)-sector slice in witness language.

## 7. Exact Seam Family Theorem

The bounded obstruction studied in this paper lives in the distinguished slot-4 slice identified above.

### Theorem 7.1. Exact seam family theorem
The distinguished slot-4 slice is exactly
\[
\Sigma_2^{(4)}
=
\{[o4,s0,t0,s2,x,s4]:
x\in\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}\}.
\]

Equivalently, every state in the distinguished slice is obtained from the exact prototype
\[
E_2=[o4,s0,t0,s2,t2,s4]
\]
by changing exactly the single coordinate \(4\).

### Corollary 7.2. Frozen-five seam law
Positions \(0,1,2,3,5\) are rigid on the distinguished slice, and only position \(4\) varies.

### Corollary 7.3. Seam-local \(O\)-support law
For every state \(c\in\Sigma_2^{(4)}\),
\[
\operatorname{Supp}_O(c)\subseteq\{o4\}.
\]

## 8. Locked Witness Dynamics and the Remaining Local Conjecture

The locked witness set satisfies
\[
L\subseteq \Sigma_2^{(4)}.
\]

Let
\[
\Sigma_2^L
\]
denote the seam states reachable from \(L\) under the retained seam dynamics.

The bounded exclusion theorem shows that throughout the bounded locked regime,
\[
\pi_4(c)\neq t2.
\]
Since the puncture condition identifies the distinguished slot-4 slice, this strongly suggests that the locked witness dynamics preserve that slice.

### Conjecture 8.1. Locked slice invariance
\[
\Sigma_2^L\subseteq \Sigma_2^{(4)}.
\]

### Stronger expected form
\[
\Sigma_2^L=\Sigma_2^{(4)}.
\]

In words: the locked witness dynamics preserve, and likely generate exactly, the unique scaffold-preserving punctured slice of the full normalized frame-2 Hamming shell.

## 9. Witness-Language Interpretation

Identify
\[
[o4,s0,t0,s2,x,s4]
\]
with the witness
\[
[W,X,Y,Z,T,I].
\]

Then
\[
W=o4,\quad X=s0,\quad Y=t0,\quad Z=s2,\quad T=x,\quad I=s4.
\]

Thus the distinguished slot-4 slice becomes a one-vertex deformation family of the witness hexagon, with \(W,X,Y,Z,I\) rigid and only \(T\) varying.

Consequently, the only variable perimeter edges are the two incident to \(T\):
\[
ZT,\qquad TI.
\]

So in witness language the bounded obstruction localizes to a single sector of an otherwise rigid scaffold.

## 10. Scope and Limits

The present result is bounded. It concerns the normalized retained substitution regime generated from the locked frame-2 witnesses.

The paper does not claim:
- a global impossibility theorem for exact frame-2 closure,
- normalization-independence,
- a derivation of the locked slice invariance conjecture directly from first principles,
- a compact invariant law implying the exact seam family without verification,
- universality beyond the chosen generating set without further proof.

These limits are part of the precision of the current theorem stack.

## 12. Conclusion

The baseline two-step policy exhibited contraction without closure, but that observation alone was too impure to support a structural claim. Exact-preference repair removed the dominant chamber-level artifact and left a sharper residual obstruction concentrated at the frame-2 seam.

The bounded retained regime confirms that this obstruction is real: the exact slot value \(t2\) never appears, and no exact frame-2 closure occurs. More importantly, the local geometry of the obstruction is now identified. The full normalized frame-2 seam is a Hamming-1 shell around the exact prototype
\[
E_2=[o4,s0,t0,s2,t2,s4],
\]
and the bounded locked regime lives in a distinguished punctured slice of that shell. That slice is exactly the one-coordinate deformation family
\[
[o4,s0,t0,s2,x,s4],
\qquad
x\in\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}.
\]

So the current achievement is not merely a computational failure to find closure. It is the identification of a precise local seam geometry: an ambient Hamming shell, a unique scaffold-preserving punctured slice, and a bounded locked regime that appears to preserve that slice.

In the shortest formulation:

> after chamber-level repair, the residual frame-2 obstruction lies in a distinguished punctured slice of the full normalized Hamming shell, and that slice is exactly the one-coordinate deformation family of the exact prototype.
