## 4. Writing principles for this paper lane

### Principle 4.1. Keep theorem voice ahead of exploratory voice
The main paper should sound like a theorem paper with an open proof program, not like a notebook of impressions.

### Principle 4.2. Separate statuses rigorously
Always distinguish:
- definitions,
- computational propositions,
- structural conjectures,
- open problems.

### Principle 4.3. Be conservative about scope
The bounded theorem is already interesting. Do not dilute it by overclaiming global impossibility.

### Principle 4.4. Let slot-4 exclusion carry the narrative
The main positive mathematical object is not mere failure of closure, but exclusion of the exact installation value at the localized obstruction coordinate.

### Principle 4.5. Use slogans sparingly
Phrases like “contraction without closure” and “the machine finds the seam without sealing it” are useful orientation devices, but should not dominate the formal sections.
