# Subjective / Objective as Lift Hypothesis

## Status
Hypothesis note

## Purpose
Place the subjective/objective distinction into the quotient tower
\[
G_{60}\to G_{30}\to G_{15}
\]
in the strongest currently plausible way.

The proposal is that subjective/objective is not natively a distinction on the compressed core \(G_{15}\), but a two-sheet distinction living above the core, most naturally at the signed-lift layer \(G_{30}\to G_{15}\).

---

## 1. Starting point

The current paper identifies three levels of structure:

- \(G_{60}\): the full 60-vertex transport graph arising from local dodecahedral transport,
- \(G_{30}\): the intermediate quotient carrying a signed 2-lift over the core,
- \(G_{15}\cong L(\mathrm{Petersen})\): the quotient core carrying the sector system, the thalion, and the quadratic law
  \[
  MM^\top = A^3 + 2A^2 + 2I.
  \]

The paper already shows that the map
\[
G_{30}\to G_{15}
\]
carries a signed 2-lift and a nontrivial \(\mathbb Z_2\)-valued cocycle. This makes the \(G_{30}\) layer the most natural candidate for any mathematically meaningful two-mode distinction. 0

---

## 2. Working hypothesis

### Hypothesis 2.1. Subjective/objective as sheet structure
The subjective/objective distinction is natively a two-sheet distinction on the transport/lift layer above the core, rather than an intrinsic distinction of the quotient core \(G_{15}\) itself.

In its strongest form:

- subjective and objective are realized as the two sheets of the lift
  \[
  G_{30}\to G_{15},
  \]
- passing to \(G_{15}\) forgets that distinction,
- but the forgetting is not total: a residual trace survives in the signed-lift cocycle and in the induced local witness behavior.

---

## 3. Why this is the right place to look

The quotient core \(G_{15}\) is already highly compressed. It is the level at which the transport system becomes algebraically legible: sector incidence, overlap geometry, spherical realization. This suggests that \(G_{15}\) is better suited to recording what survives compression than to originating the subjective/objective split itself. 1

By contrast, the paper already singles out the intermediate layer as carrying a signed 2-lift and a nontrivial cocycle. That is exactly the kind of structure in which a binary mode distinction can live geometrically rather than metaphorically. 2

So the natural doctrine is:

- subjective/objective is born above the core,
- the core remembers only its compressed residue.

---

## 4. Tower reading

Under this hypothesis, the quotient tower is read as follows.

### 4.1. \(G_{60}\): lived transport
At the top layer, the full local transport system runs. If subjective/objective is genuinely part of the machine, its most concrete operational origin is expected here.

### 4.2. \(G_{30}\): sheet memory
At the middle layer, the subjective/objective split becomes a lift distinction:
- one sheet = subjective,
- one sheet = objective.

The associated cocycle records how this sheet distinction twists along loops.

### 4.3. \(G_{15}\): compressed witness
At the core, the sheet distinction is mostly forgotten. What survives is:
- the sector incidence law,
- the quadratic overlap geometry,
- and possibly local branch/junction behavior that is the compressed witness of the higher split.

So the core is not where subjective/objective originates. It is where subjective/objective may leave readable residue after quotienting.

## 5. Relation to the local socket machine

Recent local work on the frame-2 seam yields:

- an exact local prototype,
- a full Hamming-1 seam shell,
- an exact payload junction,
- a closed punctured socket branch.

This local machine lives on the compressed core \(G_{15}\). Under the present hypothesis, it should not yet be regarded as the full subjective/objective machine. Rather, it is the quotient-visible readout of a higher two-sheet transport distinction.

Thus the right question becomes:

> Is the local socket/junction picture on \(G_{15}\) the quotient shadow of a subjective/objective two-sheet distinction at \(G_{30}\)?

This is the key bridge question.

## 6. Strong form of the hypothesis

### Hypothesis 6.1. Subjective/objective lift doctrine
There exists a canonical identification of the two sheets of the lift
\[
G_{30}\to G_{15}
\]
with subjective and objective transport modes such that:

1. the sheet swap is the subjective/objective toggle,
2. the quotient map to \(G_{15}\) forgets the toggle,
3. the signed-lift cocycle measures nontrivial transport of this toggle around loops,
4. the local witness/socket behavior on \(G_{15}\) is the compressed readout of the two-sheet machine.

## 8. What is still missing

Nothing in the current theorem stack yet proves this doctrine.

The signed-lift cocycle is established, and the local socket machine is established, but the two have not yet been formally connected.

So this note is deliberately a hypothesis note, not a theorem note.
