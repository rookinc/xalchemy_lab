# Abstract

## Version
Working abstract

We study a localized obstruction phenomenon in the normalized G900 witness machine. Under the baseline two-step policy, the machine is monotone in normalized action distance on the sampled run but produces no nontrivial exact closure, exhibiting contraction without closure. An exact-preference repair resolves the dominant reachable chamber-level failure, showing that a substantial part of the original obstruction was due to policy selection rather than deep global impossibility.

After this repair, the residual basin funnels into the frame-2 near-action seam, where the remaining mismatch localizes to a single normalized coordinate, slot 4. Exact frame-2 closure requires slot 4 to take the value \(t2\). Starting from a distinguished locked subset of frame-2-nearest witnesses, we define a bounded escape-return regime generated by legal one-edit moves subject to the cutoff \(d_A \le 3\). This regime saturates computationally by depth 6, with no new states at depths 7 and 8, and contains 58,624 visited states.

Within this saturated bounded regime, no exact frame-2 closure appears. More strongly, the exact slot-4 value \(t2\) never appears anywhere in the regime. On the frame-2 seam itself, the observed slot-4 alphabet is
\[
\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\},
\]
again excluding \(t2\). These results support a bounded slot-4 exclusion law and a bounded no-closure corollary for frame 2.

We then formulate a structural proof program for explaining this exclusion. The main proposed route is to derive the projected slot-4 transport relation induced by the one-edit grammar and normalization law, prove closure of the reachable seam alphabet, and show that \(t2\) lies outside that closed transport set. Candidate deeper explanations include affine residue obstructions, normalization-orbit restrictions, and a defect-transport interpretation in which bounded one-edits move a localized slot-4 defect without annihilating it.

The paper makes a bounded theorem claim, not a global impossibility claim. Its main contribution is to sharpen a diffuse closure failure into a localized seam obstruction and to identify bounded slot-4 exclusion as the central theorem target.
