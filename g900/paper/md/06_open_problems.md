# Open Problems for the G900 Witness-Machine Obstruction Program

## Status
Working draft

## Purpose
Record the main unresolved problems exposed by the bounded frame-2 obstruction result, with clear separation between bounded theorem targets, structural conjectures, and possible global extensions.

---

## 1. Immediate theorem problem

### Problem 1.1. Prove bounded slot-4 exclusion structurally
Let \(B\) be the bounded escape-return regime generated from the locked frame-2 witnesses under legal one-edit moves with action-distance cutoff \(d_A \le 3\).

Prove:
\[
\forall c \in B,\quad \pi_4(c)\neq t2.
\]

This is the central open theorem problem.

At present, it is established computationally but not yet derived from the move grammar, normalization law, or invariant structure.

---

## 2. Projected transport problem

### Problem 2.1. Derive the projected slot-4 transport relation
Construct the induced slot-4 transition relation
\[
a \rightsquigarrow_4 b
\]
from the legal one-edit grammar and normalization law.

The goal is to determine whether the bounded slot-4 dynamics can be proved on the projected alphabet alone.

### Problem 2.2. Prove closure of the seam alphabet
Show that the set
\[
A_4^{\mathrm{seam}}=\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}
\]
is closed under the projected slot-4 transport law on the bounded frame-2 seam.

### Problem 2.3. Prove minimality
Show that \(A_4^{\mathrm{seam}}\) is the minimal closed slot-4 transport set generated by the locked-seed slot values
\[
\{t4,o4,s3,s0\}.
\]

This would make the seam alphabet a genuine mathematical object rather than an observed list.

## 3. Invariant problems

### Problem 3.1. Find a parity-like invariant, if one exists
Determine whether there is a nontrivial \(\mathbb{Z}/2\)-valued invariant preserved by bounded legal one-edits and normalization which excludes \(t2\).

A naive index-parity law appears too weak, but a family-index or normalization-corrected parity may still exist.

### Problem 3.2. Test affine residue laws modulo 5
Determine whether there is a preserved affine residue of the form
\[
R(c)\equiv \sum_{j=0}^{5}\alpha_j\,\mathrm{ind}(x_j)+\sum_{j=0}^{5}\beta_j\,\mathrm{fam}(x_j)\pmod 5
\]
or a related cyclic law that explains the exclusion of \(t2\) from slot 4 in the bounded regime.

### Problem 3.3. Relate any invariant to normalization
If a parity or residue law exists, determine whether it is intrinsic to the move grammar or induced only after passing to normalized representatives.

---

## 4. Orbit and normalization problems

### Problem 4.1. Identify the relevant normalization action
Make explicit the normalization action or quotient structure under which the witness states are represented.

### Problem 4.2. Determine whether \(t2\) lies in a distinct orbit slice
Test whether the excluded value \(t2\) belongs to a different orbit, coset, or representative class than the observed bounded seam alphabet.

### Problem 4.3. Pre-normalized versus normalized accessibility
Determine whether a pre-normalized local edit can formally create a slot-4 \(t2\)-type value which is then destroyed, reindexed, or expelled by normalization.

This would clarify whether the exclusion is fundamentally grammatical or quotient-theoretic.

---

## 5. Defect-transport problems

### Problem 5.1. Define the slot-4 defect formally
Give a precise definition of the residual slot-4 defect after exact-preference repair.

The defect should not merely mean “wrong symbol,” but should identify the installation failure class that remains invariant or nearly invariant under bounded legal one-edits.

### Problem 5.2. Prove defect transport without annihilation
Show that bounded legal one-edits transport the slot-4 defect among seam-visible representatives but do not annihilate it.

### Problem 5.3. Identify \(t2\) with exact defect absence
Prove that \(t2\) at slot 4 corresponds to exact installation rather than another defect presentation.

This would support the conceptual reading that the machine transports the defect but does not resolve it.

---

## 6. Boundary-of-boundedness problems

### Problem 6.1. Is action distance 3 the true barrier?
Determine whether the bound \(d_A \le 3\) is a merely convenient cutoff or a genuine structural threshold.

### Problem 6.2. Escape requirement for \(t2\)
Test the conjecture:

If a path from the locked witness set \(L\) reaches a state with \(\pi_4=t2\), then that path must pass through a state with
\[
d_A(\,\cdot\,,F_2^{\mathrm{exact}})\ge 4.
\]

This would turn bounded exclusion into a barrier theorem.

### Problem 6.3. First escape geometry
If escape beyond the bounded regime is necessary, determine the first geometric mode by which such escape occurs:
- chamber jump,
- orbit shift,
- normalization break,
- multi-slot correlated repair,
- defect annihilation via nonlocal move sequence.

## 7. Seam geometry problems

### Problem 7.1. Why this seam alphabet?
Explain structurally why the frame-2 seam alphabet is
\[
\{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}
\]
rather than some other subset.

### Problem 7.2. Why is \(t2\) uniquely excluded?
Show why \(t2\) is singled out among natural same-family candidates.

### Problem 7.3. Global versus seam-local slot-4 alphabet
Explain the difference between:
- the global bounded slot-4 alphabet
  \[
  \{o0,o1,o2,o3,o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\},
  \]
- the seam slot-4 alphabet
  \[
  \{o4,s0,s2,s3,s4,t0,t3,t4\}.
  \]

The distinction suggests that seam localization imposes a sharper transport law than the ambient bounded regime.

## 10. Current recommended priority order

The current recommended order of attack is:

### Priority 1
Derive the projected slot-4 transport relation from the one-edit grammar and normalization law.

### Priority 2
Prove closure of the seam alphabet under the projected transport law.

### Priority 3
Prove nonproduction of \(t2\) from the closed seam alphabet.

### Priority 4
Search for a deeper affine-residue, orbit, or defect-transport explanation.

### Priority 5
Only after the bounded theorem is structurally secure, explore whether reaching \(t2\) requires escape beyond \(d_A \le 3\).

## 11. Present working summary

The immediate mathematical opportunity is no longer to search larger state regions blindly.

It is to explain why the bounded frame-2 seam dynamics live in a proper slot-4 transport subalphabet that excludes the exact installation value \(t2\).

That is the cleanest next theorem target.
