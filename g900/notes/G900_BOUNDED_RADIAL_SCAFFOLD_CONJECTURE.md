## A short note on inner datum, outer landmark, and the cosmological bracket of the climb

Date: 2026-03-29

---

## Purpose

This note records a conjectural radial framing for the project.

It does not claim a theorem.
It does not claim a measured derivation.
It does not claim that the full bridge program has already been solved.

It records a conjecture strong enough to orient the climb:

> the project may live inside a bounded radial scaffold whose inner datum is \(\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}\) and whose outer summit marker is \(r_{\max}\), defined as the distance from here to the CMB.

This note exists so that the conjecture is preserved cleanly without being over-promoted.

## 1. The conjecture

The conjecture is that the project admits a bounded radial frame of the form

\[
\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}} \;\longrightarrow\; r_{\max}
\]

where:

- \(\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}\) is the inner datum
- \(r_{\max}\) is the outer landmark
- and \(r_{\max}\) is taken, provisionally, as the distance from here to the CMB

This gives the project a cosmological bracket.

Not merely an abstract scale range.
A bounded radial scaffold.

## 4. Why this remains a conjecture

This note intentionally uses the word **conjecture**.

Why?

Because the project does not yet have:

- a proven derivation from admissible action to cosmological radius
- a theorem identifying G900 directly with a Planck-to-CMB radial law
- a finished operator connecting the inner datum to the outer marker
- a demonstrated unique way to interpret all intermediate structure on this interval

What the project does have is a strong orientation claim:
that the lawful climb is not radially unbounded in concept, but bracketed.

That is enough to guide search without pretending completion.

That distinction matters.
