# Post-30 Ascent Operator

## Status
Working note

## Purpose

This note defines the smallest nontrivial machine beyond the locked
15 -> 30 walker cycle.

The key idea is:

- 15 gives the sign hinge
- 30 gives restored identity
- after 30, the next live structure is not mere repetition
- it is the orthogonal companion sector of the restored relaunch space

So the first post-30 ascent is defined on the orthogonal pair.

---

## 1. Locked first-order machine

The first-order walker law is:

\[
W(x,\varepsilon,h)=(x,-\varepsilon,\phi(h))
\]

with Type III:

\[
\phi(0)=0,\quad \phi(2)=2,\quad \phi(1)=3,\quad \phi(3)=1.
\]

Thus:

\[
W(Q_0 n)=-(Q_0 n)
\]
\[
W(Q_{180} n)=-(Q_{180} n)
\]
\[
W(Q_{90} n)=-(Q_{270} n)
\]
\[
W(Q_{270} n)=-(Q_{90} n).
\]

---

## 2. Orthogonal companion sector

Define the orthogonal sector over a restored state \(n\) by:

\[
\mathcal O(n):=\{Q_{90}n,\ Q_{270}n\}.
\]

This is the active companion sector of the Type III relaunch machine.

---

## 3. Ascent operator

Define the post-30 ascent operator \(A\) by restricting the one-cycle law to the orthogonal sector:

\[
A := W\big|_{\mathcal O}.
\]

So:

\[
A(Q_{90}n)=-(Q_{270}n)
\]
\[
A(Q_{270}n)=-(Q_{90}n).
\]

This is the smallest nontrivial post-30 operator.

It flips sign and exchanges companion class.

---

## 4. Two-step return inside the orthogonal sector

Applying \(A\) twice restores the orthogonal launch state:

\[
A^2(Q_{90}n)=Q_{90}n
\]
\[
A^2(Q_{270}n)=Q_{270}n.
\]

So the orthogonal companion sector is a two-step machine under the ascent operator.

---

## 5. Stabilization map

The ascent operator alone does not yet produce a new world.

So introduce a stabilization map:

\[
\Sigma : \mathcal O(n)^\ast \to Y
\]

where \(Y\) is a set of host-stabilization classes.

Current working hypothesis:

\[
\Sigma(\text{completed orthogonal companion exchange})=9.
\]

That is the current strongest candidate bridge from restored relaunch dynamics to the first local world layer.

---

## 6. First recursive extension

Once stabilization occurs, the resulting world supports the familiar walker ladder:

\[
9 \to 15 \to 30.
\]

So the first recursive ascent beyond the original walker cycle is:

1. restored walker at 30
2. orthogonal companion exchange
3. stabilization into 9
4. fresh transformation at 15
5. fresh restoration at 30

This is the first true extension beyond simple repetition.

---

## 7. One-line summary

Beyond 30, the machine steps up by acting on the orthogonal companion sector of restored relaunch space, and the completed stabilization of that sector is the first candidate source of a new world layer.
