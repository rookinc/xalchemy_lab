## 1. Scope

This note records the present interpretation of the depth-2 basin exploration around the canonical witness families.

Does the one-edit local geometry persist when the seed is perturbed by two edits instead of one?

- a robust subjective basin
- a robust objective basin

The depth-2 scan was motivated by the following concern:

A one-edit local picture can sometimes be misleading. A family may look stable under one small perturbation and then collapse under two edits.

So the key question was:

At depth 2, do we still see the same three-basin geometry, or do new direct tunnels appear?

The present answer is:

The same geometry remains visible, and the previously observed mechanisms become clearer rather than weaker.

## 4. Main qualitative result

The current depth-2 objective scan continues to show three recurring outcomes:

1. objective-state nearest
2. action-cell nearest
3. unresolved ambiguous boundary cases

What is not presently standing out in the inspected objective depth-2 examples is a clean direct migration into subjective-state.

That absence matters.

It means the current depth-2 evidence still supports the idea that the objective basin does not directly tunnel into subjective under ordinary double perturbation in the explored local vocabulary.

Instead, the basin continues to fail in two main ways:

- toward the ambiguity ridge
- toward the action family

That is exactly the pattern already seen at depth 1.

The difference is that depth 2 thickens the pattern and makes the channels easier to recognize.

## 5. What depth 2 adds

Depth 2 does not merely repeat depth 1. It clarifies the mechanism.

At depth 1, one could say:

- shell damage tends to feed action
- hinge damage tends to feed ambiguity

At depth 2, this becomes more convincing because doubly damaged cycles repeatedly organize into the same outcomes.

### 5.1 Shell/species damage strengthens action attraction
Many two-edit perturbations of the form

- convert one outer O slot into S or T
- and deform a neighboring O slot or adjacent symbol

land as:

- action-cell
- nearest

often with best_action_distance = 2

This matters because it shows the action spillway is not a one-edit accident. It persists as a depth-2 deformation channel.

In other words:

action is not only the place where state seeds go when they are nicked once; it is also the place they continue to go when the shell grammar is doubly damaged.

### 5.2 Hinge damage continues to feed ambiguity
Depth-2 examples that mix the hinge coordinates in balanced ways continue to produce unresolved ambiguous cases.

This means the hinge ambiguity ridge is not disappearing as perturbation depth increases. It is broadening.

So the state/state boundary is not a single fragile knife-edge. It has thickness.

This is exactly what one would expect from a real local boundary geometry.

### 5.3 Core-preserving deformations remain objective
Many double edits that still preserve enough of the objective backbone remain:

- objective-state
- nearest

This is equally important.

It says the objective basin still has a coherent chamber at depth 2. It has not dissolved into unresolved mush.

So the objective family remains a real local attractor even after two edits.

## 6. Emerging geometric picture

At this point the local basin morphology is starting to look stable across perturbation depth.

The current best picture is:

- subjective chamber
- objective chamber
- action chamber
- an ambiguity seam between the two state chambers
- shell-damage channels feeding action
- action/objective adjacency stronger than action/subjective adjacency

Another way to say it:

- state/state separation is controlled by hinge coordinates
- state/action separation is controlled by species and shell placement
- action behaves like a distinct third family, but not like a perfectly central one

That last clause remains important.

Nothing in the present depth-2 read suggests that action is symmetrically centered between subjective and objective.

Instead, the evidence still points toward:

- action as a genuine third attractor
- but with a preferred adjacency to objective structure

## 10. What the machine is now teaching us

At this stage the machine seems to be revealing two different geometric mechanisms, and depth 2 makes that distinction stronger.

### Mechanism A: hinge ambiguity
When the hinge coordinates are mixed across the subjective/objective split, the result tends toward unresolved ambiguity.

This is a state-boundary phenomenon.

### Mechanism B: shell/species failure
When the shell grammar is damaged, especially at the O positions, the result tends toward action structure.

This is a state/action phenomenon.

These are not the same failure mode.

That is one of the strongest lessons of the local scan program so far.
