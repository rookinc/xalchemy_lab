# G Compression Stack from G30

## Status
Working compression note
Aggressive reduction from the current G15/G30 ladder

## Purpose

This note records the result of a deliberate compression attempt.

The question driving it is:

> If we squeeze the current G15/G30 machinery as hard as possible, what survives as primitive, what reduces cleanly, and what remains genuinely higher-order?

The point is not to force everything into a tiny formula.
The point is to see which structures remain stable under compression.

This note therefore starts from the current strongest ladder and repeatedly reduces it until only the most durable pieces remain.

---

## 1. Starting ladder

The current strongest working ladder is:

\[
5 \rightsquigarrow 4
\]
\[
20 \rightsquigarrow 9
\]
\[
9 \to 15 \to 30
\]

with the provisional meanings:

- \(4\) = frame
- \(9\) = world
- \(15\) = transformation / sign hinge
- \(30\) = restored identity

This is the starting point for compression.

---

## 2. First compression: separate clocks

The first reduction move is to separate the two clocks:

### Edge / transport side
\[
5,\ 20
\]

### Vertex / walker side
\[
4,\ 9,\ 15,\ 30
\]

This already reveals an important fact:

- the transport ladder and the walker ladder are not the same thing
- higher-order structure appears on the walker side
- the transport side seems to support it

This means the vertex-side ladder is the better candidate for structural compression.

So the first reduced walker ladder is:

\[
4 \to 9 \to 15 \to 30
\]

---

## 3. Second compression: reduce G30

The current locked machine is:

\[
n_{15}=-n_0
\]
\[
n_{30}=n_0
\]

So \(30\) is not an independent first object.
It is the second application of the \(15\)-law.

Thus:

\[
30 = 2 \cdot 15
\]

in the structural sense of “two sign-hinge traversals produce full restoration.”

So the first major compression is:

\[
G30 \leadsto 2\cdot G15
\]

This means G30 is real and important, but not primitive.

It is the first doubled hinge, not the first kernel.

---

## 4. Third compression: reduce G15

There is a standing intuition that:

\[
15 = 5 \times 3
\]

This is not yet fully proven, but it is the strongest current compression candidate for \(15\).

The intended meanings are:

- \(5\) = grounded/apex seed
- \(3\) = transport kernel

So under compression:

\[
G15 \leadsto G5 \times G3
\]

Meaning:

> the first walker sign-hinge arises from the grounded seed carried through the ternary transport kernel.

This is the boldest compression step in the note.
It is elegant and currently plausible, but also the least settled of the main identities.

So it should be treated as a strong working conjecture, not yet as a theorem.

Still, it survives compression better than expected.

---

## 5. Fourth compression: reduce G9

The best current read of \(9\) is:

\[
9 = 1 + 4 + 4
\]

Interpretation:

- one center
- four primary sector directions
- four mirrored, returned, or paired supports

So:

\[
G9 \leadsto G1 + G4 + G4
\]

This is a very attractive reduction because it matches the idea of a first stabilized local world:

- center present
- frame present
- frame completed or reflected

So \(9\) is not primitive either.
It is the first world-scale stabilization built from smaller pieces.

---

## 6. Fifth compression: reduce G5

This step is very strong.

The current two best equivalent readings of \(5\) are:

### Grounded cross form
\[
5 = 1 + 4
\]

one center plus four equidistant cardinal companions

### Apex-triangle form
four triangles meeting at a common apex

These are not competing descriptions.
They are two realizations of the same grounded seed.

So the compressed identity is:

\[
G5 \leadsto G1 + G4
\]

This is one of the strongest surviving relations in the whole stack.

These five levels survive the squeeze as the most primitive stack currently visible.

## 11. Hardest pressure point

The current weakest link in the compressed chain is still:

\[
G15 \approx G5 \times G3
\]

Why?

Because it is conceptually elegant and plausible, but not yet as directly grounded as:

\[
G5 = 1+4
\]

or

\[
G30 = 2\cdot 15.
\]

So if anything in the current compression stack later proves too ambitious, this is the first place to revisit.

That should be stated explicitly to keep the note honest.
