# G900 k = 1 and Planck Anchor Note
## A short note on epistemic root and cosmological datum

Date: 2026-03-29

---

## Purpose

This note records an important distinction for the project.

The project is cosmological in scope.
Its motivation is philosophical.
But its physical anchor is not left floating.

The correct placement is:

- \(k = 1\) remains the **epistemic root**
- \(\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}\) is the **physical anchor / datum**

This note exists to keep those roles distinct without separating them so far that the project loses coherence.

---

## 1. The root

The declaration

\[
k = 1
\]

should remain the root condition of knowability.

In the project language inherited from the executable-knowledge note, this means:

- the minimal admissible distinction
- the smallest epistemic step
- the root from which lawful construction begins

It is not, in this role, a measured length.
It is not a cosmological constant.
It is not a claim about ontology by itself.

It is a boundary condition on executable knowability.

So the project should continue to treat \(k = 1\) as the root declaration.

---

## 2. The anchor

The project also needs a physical datum.

Because the scope is cosmological, the project should not pretend that all realizations are equally anchored.
A physical frame is needed.
For this project, that frame is:

\[
\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}
\]

This is the anchor scale.
The datum.
The smallest physically privileged scale at which the cosmological realization is to be read.

So while \(k = 1\) remains epistemic, \(\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}\) is the physical anchor for realization.

That is the correct division.

---

## 3. Why the distinction matters

If the project simply says

\[
k = \ell_{\mathrm{Planck}},
\]

it risks collapsing two levels that should remain distinct:

- the condition for knowability
- the physical unit of cosmological anchoring

Those are related, but they are not identical.

The project becomes stronger, not weaker, by preserving the distinction.

### Why it is stronger

Because then the project can say:

- the epistemic root is minimal admissible distinction
- the physical realization is Planck-anchored
- the lawful climb from root to image is not arbitrary
- but neither is it reduced immediately to a unit convention

This preserves both:
- philosophical depth
- and cosmological seriousness

---

## 4. The project stack under this decision

The stack should now be read as:

### Epistemic root
\[
k = 1
\]

### Physical datum
\[
\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}
\]

### Generator
The lawful progression that walks admissible action.

### Carrier
The resulting lawful object or state stream.

### Lens
The structured readout through which lawful output becomes legible.

### Image
The visible form extracted through the lens.

### Law
The stable compression of that image.

This is the right order.

---

## 5. Interpretation rule

A good interpretation rule is:

> one admissible step is not identical to Planck length, but the project reads admissible progression against a Planck-anchored cosmological frame.

That is subtle, but important.

It means:
- the root is epistemic
- the anchor is physical
- the project is allowed to be cosmological
- the generator is allowed to be lawful rather than merely metrical
- and the later images and laws may still be read against a physical datum

This is exactly the kind of distinction the project needs.

---

## 6. What this allows

This decision allows the project to say all of the following without contradiction:

### 6.1
The project is philosophically motivated.

### 6.2
The project is cosmological in scope.

### 6.3
The root of knowability is procedural and admissible rather than metrical.

### 6.4
The physical realization is not left unanchored, but is read against Planck length as datum.

### 6.5
The later bridge program may search for laws that are invariant in image space while still being physically interpretable through a Planck-anchored frame.

That is a very strong combination.

---

## 7. What this does not claim

This note does **not** claim:

- that \(k = 1\) is numerically equal to \(\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}\)
- that the admissible-action generator is already a physical theory of Planck-scale spacetime
- that every lawful image must reduce immediately to Planck-scale geometry
- that the cosmological summit is already proved by choosing the right datum

Those would be overclaims.

The note claims only that the project now has:
- an epistemic root
- and a physical anchor

and that both are necessary.

---

## 8. Best short formulation

The cleanest short formulation is:

> In this project, \(k = 1\) is the root condition of knowability, while \(\ell_{\mathrm{Planck}}\) is the physical datum that anchors the cosmological realization.

A slightly stronger formulation is:

> The project begins from executable distinction and is anchored physically at Planck length.

Both should be preserved.

---

## 9. Closing orientation

This distinction clarifies the mountain.

Basecamp is not pure philosophy.
Basecamp is not yet full cosmology either.

It is the first place where:

- admissible distinction has been declared,
- lawful generation has begun,
- and the climb has been anchored to a physical datum.

That is enough.
