Action Two-Step Repair Motifs
Date: 2026-03-31

Status
Working note based on targeted oracle analysis of the disputed action-cell depth-4 starts.

Purpose
Record the strongest current local-grammar conclusion:
the decisive repair structure appears to be short-horizon paired motifs rather than a scalar first-move rule.

Context
Earlier stages established:

1. The action-cell basin is real.
2. Repair anisotropy is real.
3. Raw distance is informative but incomplete.
4. Support class and seam/sector structure explain part of the behavior.
5. A naive weak-sector penalty does not improve recovery on matched samples.
6. Targeted first-move oracle analysis showed many admissible first moves exist for the disputed starts.

This note records the next rung:
successful local recovery is organized by two-step repair motifs.

Main conclusion
The disputed action-cell starts are not merely recoverable.
They are recoverable by short paired repair motifs.

The decisive local structure is therefore not:

- raw distance alone
- support class alone
- first-move feature alone

Instead, the current evidence points to:

a short-horizon repair grammar in which the first move must participate in a valid second-step completion pattern.

Key finding
For each of the targeted delta starts, there exist two-step motifs that lead back to exact action-cell recovery.

In several cases, the motif is effectively order-flexible:
A then B works, and B then A also works.

This strongly suggests that some local repairs are organized as paired coordinate corrections rather than as a single rigid one-step rule.

Representative motifs

1. Support 0|1|3|4
Transition:
0:o2->o3 | 1:s2->s3 | 3:s0->t3 | 4:t3->s0

Observed motifs:
- first 0:o3->o2 then 1:s3->s2
- first 0:o3->s2 then 1:s3->o2
- first 0:o3->t3 then 3:t3->s2

Interpretation:
This start admits a small family of two-step repairs that correct two damaged coordinates in succession.
Some variants restore directly toward the seed witness, others reconfigure first and normalize second.

2. Support 0|2|3|5
Transition:
0:o2->s2 | 2:t0->s0 | 3:s0->o3 | 5:s3->s0

Observed motifs:
- first 0:s2->t0 then 3:o3->s3
- first 2:s0->o2 then 3:o3->s3
- first 3:o3->s3 then 0:s2->t0

Interpretation:
This is a strong example of an order-flexible repair pair.
The moves on positions 0 and 3 can close in either order.

3. Support 0|1|3|5
Transition:
0:o2->s3 | 1:s2->o3 | 3:s0->t4 | 5:s3->o3

Observed motifs:
- first 0:s3->o2 then 3:t4->s0
- first 3:t4->s0 then 0:s3->o2
- first 5:o3->s3 then 0:s3->o2

Interpretation:
Again we see paired local correction, and again one pair is order-flexible.
The repair grammar is not limited to one anchor position.

4. Support 0|3|4|5
Transition:
0:o2->s3 | 3:s0->s4 | 4:t3->s2 | 5:s3->o3

Observed motifs:
- first 0:s3->o2 then 5:o3->s3
- first 1:s2->t3 then 2:t0->s0
- first 2:t0->s0 then 1:s2->t3

Interpretation:
This is especially important because this support class appeared difficult in earlier aggregate scans.
Yet even here, short paired motifs exist, including a clearly order-flexible pair on positions 1 and 2.

5. Support 0|1|3|4
Transition:
0:o2->t3 | 1:s2->o1 | 3:s0->o0 | 4:t3->s0

Observed motifs:
- first 1:o1->s0 then 3:o0->s2
- first 3:o0->s2 then 1:o1->s0
- first 1:o1->s2 then 3:o0->s2

Interpretation:
This is another strong order-flexible paired repair case.

6. Support 0|2|3|5
Transition:
0:o2->t3 | 2:t0->s2 | 3:s0->t3 | 5:s3->o4

Observed motifs:
- first 0:t3->o2 then 5:o4->s3
- first 0:t3->t0 then 2:s2->o2
- first 2:s2->o2 then 0:t3->t0

Interpretation:
The start admits more than one distinct paired motif family, suggesting local redundancy in the repair fan-out.

7. Support 0|2|3|4
Transition:
0:o2->t3 | 2:t0->t4 | 3:s0->o3 | 4:t3->s2

Observed motifs:
- first 0:t3->o2 then 2:t4->t0
- first 1:s2->s0 then 4:s2->o2
- first 2:t4->t0 then 0:t3->o2

Interpretation:
This again shows both direct seedward correction and order-flexible paired repair.

What the motifs suggest

A. Two-coordinate correction is the dominant local unit
Many successful repairs are not isolated single edits.
They are paired corrections on two coordinates.

B. Order-flexibility appears in multiple cases
For several starts, A then B works and B then A works.
This suggests weak coupling or partial independence between certain local repair channels.

C. First-step quality is relational, not absolute
A first move is not “good” in isolation.
It is good insofar as it belongs to a valid two-step completion pattern.

D. Seed restoration is neither necessary nor sufficient at step 1
Some successful motifs restore seed labels immediately.
Others do not.
So the grammar cannot be reduced to “always restore the seed first.”

E. The real control law is short-horizon
The current evidence strongly suggests that the missing policy invariant is at least two-step.
A purely myopic first-move rule is too weak.

Best current statement
The local repair grammar of the action-cell witness is organized by short paired motifs.
In the analyzed depth-4 delta starts, exact recovery is frequently achieved by two-step correction pairs, often with order flexibility.
This shows that the decisive policy object is not a scalar first-move property, but participation in a valid short-horizon repair motif.

Implications
1. Basin failures under current greedy policies are policy failures, not absence of repair paths.
2. Support class and anisotropy are explanatory but not sufficient for control.
3. The next useful policy design should score candidate first moves by whether they are likely to admit a strong second-step completion.

Best next experiments

1. Build a two-step motif scorer
Estimate whether a candidate first move has a high-quality second-step continuation.

2. Compare order-flexible versus order-rigid motifs
Check whether commutative repair pairs dominate the observed successful motifs.

3. Cluster motifs by coordinate pair
Identify which coordinate-pair corrections recur most often.

4. Test motif-aware greedy policy
Replace one-step greedy descent with a shallow two-step lookahead restricted to promising pairs.

Working conclusion
The action-cell witness is anisotropic but locally rich in admissible exits.
The currently observed local repair law is best understood as a short-horizon paired repair grammar, not as a scalar first-move rule.
