# G900 Lens Note
## A working note on carrier, lens, image, law, residue, and admissible action

Date: 2026-03-29

---

## 0. Why this note exists

This note exists to stabilize a piece of language that now feels both useful and earned.

The current G900 schema work is not merely a software convenience.
It is not merely a data format.
It is not merely a bookkeeping layer.
And it is not yet the theorem, not yet the object, and not yet the law itself.

What it appears to be is a **lens**.

That word matters because it clarifies several distinctions at once:

- the distinction between the thing and the way it is seen
- the distinction between raw growth and structured observation
- the distinction between a carrier and the lawful image extracted from it
- the distinction between what is present in the object and what becomes visible under a chosen mode of observation

This note is therefore a vocabulary note, but not a decorative one.
It is an attempt to preserve a clean conceptual stack before the project grows more machinery and the terminology hardens in a confused way.

The key claim of this note is simple:

> The current strict G900 binning/schema layer is best understood as the kernel of a lens.

Not the carrier.
Not the law.
Not the observer in the full sense.
But the lens through which lawful image may become extractable.

---

## 1. The basic distinction

The project needs a clean separation between at least five things:

1. the **carrier**
2. the **lens**
3. the **image**
4. the **law**
5. the **residue**

Without this separation, everything collapses into mush:
the graph is mistaken for the theorem,
the data model is mistaken for the object,
the observation protocol is mistaken for the action law,
and interpretive language outruns the finite structure.

So the first purpose of the lens language is hygienic.
It prevents category collapse.

The second purpose is generative.
It gives us a stable way to talk about how lawful visibility emerges from a raw grown object.

---

## 2. Carrier

### Working definition

A **carrier** is the underlying object that bears the structure under study.

In the G900 setting, the carrier may be:

- the full grown graph
- the chamber or cell complex
- the transport body
- the growth record
- the quotient tower taken as a whole
- or some layered package consisting of growth object plus induced quotient structure

The important point is that the carrier is what exists **prior to observation through a chosen lens**.

The carrier is not the summary.
Not the histogram.
Not the shell table.
Not the overlap operator.
Not the residue table.
Not the spectral plot.

It is the thing that can in principle be observed in multiple ways.

### Why the word helps

“Carrier” is better than simply saying “graph” when the object may eventually include:

- typed transport roles
- chamber memory
- growth epochs
- quotient maps
- cocycle or parity structure
- induced operators
- or geometric embedding data

A plain graph may be one presentation of the carrier, but the carrier can be richer than any single presentation.

### For G900

At current scope, the safest phrasing is:

> G900 is the carrier, or at least the primary carrier under current study.

That leaves room for the possibility that the true carrier is not merely a 900-vertex graph, but a larger structured package containing growth, transport, and descent data.

---

## 3. Lens

### Working definition

A **lens** is a structured mode of observation that makes selected features of the carrier legible.

This is the central term.

A lens does not create the carrier.
It does not necessarily exhaust the carrier.
It does not prove the law by itself.
It does not choose goals in the full observer sense.

What it does is:

- define what is being measured
- define how it is grouped
- define what distinctions are visible
- define what gets compressed
- define what counts as same, similar, or different under the current mode of observation

So the lens is not the object.
It is the lawful structure of visibility.

### Why this word is better than “observer”

“Observer” is too strong and too blurry at once.

Too strong because it suggests agency, judgment, intention, maybe even consciousness.
Too blurry because it mixes together stance, memory, criteria, protocol, and instrument.

“Lens” is cleaner.

It says:
there is a carrier,
there is a way of looking,
and that way of looking has structure.

That is exactly what the strict schema is doing.

### The current schema as lens kernel

The strict G900 schema specifies:

- what bins exist
- what observables belong to which bins
- which dimensions of the object are to be recorded
- which summaries are canonical
- which field names and categories are lawful
- how strictness is enforced
- how drift is constrained

That is not yet a full lens in the richest possible sense, because a full lens may also require:

- root choice
- quotient choice
- normalization choice
- residue thresholds
- admissibility thresholds
- mode-selection policies
- update rules over time

But it is already the kernel of the lens.

Hence the strongest safe phrasing is:

> The strict G900 schema is the kernel of the G900 lens.

Or, slightly fuller:

> The strict G900 schema is the kernel of a formal observational lens for lawful structure on the G900 carrier.

## 4. Image

### Working definition

An **image** is what becomes visible through a lens.

If the carrier is the thing and the lens is the mode of observation, then the image is the resulting structured appearance.

Examples of images on G900 include:

- shell occupancy profiles
- shell transition tables
- growth-generation histograms
- transport-role distributions
- pair-distance overlap tables
- angular density maps
- quotient fiber summaries
- residue maps
- spectral-band participation summaries

These are not the carrier itself.
They are not necessarily the law itself.
They are images.

### Why this matters

The same carrier can yield different images under different lenses.

A shell lens may produce a radial growth image.
A quotient lens may produce an effective core image.
A spectral lens may produce a low-mode closure image.
A cocycle lens may produce a parity-memory image.

This is powerful because it allows multiple lawful views without forcing premature unification.

The project does not need to insist that one image is the whole truth.
It only needs to keep track of which lens produced which image.

### Strong phrasing

> The image is the structured appearance of the carrier under a chosen lens.

That is likely a keeper definition.

---

## 5. Law

### Working definition

A **law** is a stable relation visible through one or more lenses.

Law is not just any pattern.
Not just a pretty plot.
Not just a visual impression.
Not just a temporary numerical regularity.

A law is something that holds stably and compresses the image in a meaningful way.

Examples, in general form, would be:

- a shell recurrence
- a quotient identity
- a polynomial overlap law
- a constrained spectral relation
- a parity conservation
- a closure condition
- an admissibility criterion stable across a whole family of actions

A law may be visible only through a particular lens.
That does not make it unreal.
It only means the law is lens-relative in visibility, even if carrier-relative in truth.

### Lens-relative visibility

This is a crucial point.

Some laws do not show up under raw visualization.
Some do not show up under shell counts.
Some do not show up under adjacency alone.
Some do not show up under local neighborhoods.
Some appear only after quotienting, centering, normalizing, or coarse-graining.

So “law” must be distinguished from “immediately obvious feature.”

A law is often what becomes visible only after the right lens has been constructed.

This gives the lens concept real weight.
A good lens is not cosmetic.
It may be the very condition under which the law can appear.

---

## 6. Residue

### Working definition

**Residue** is what remains unmatched, excessive, defective, or not yet compressed by the active law under the active lens.

This is the right place to preserve your instinct that one should not immediately say “collapse.”
Residue is not merely failure.
It is informative remainder.

Residue can mean:

- deviation from expected shell occupancy
- mismatch from an ideal overlap law
- anomalous transport-role behavior
- quotient-fragile structure
- local defect
- parity excess
- incomplete closure
- uncompressed mode content

### Why residue matters

If you do not name residue, then every deviation gets treated as either:

- noise
- error
- or embarrassment

That is too crude.

In a lawful exploratory project, residue is often exactly where the next structure hides.

The right question is not always:
“why did this fail?”
It is often:
“what does the residue still carry that the current law has not yet absorbed?”

That makes residue a first-class citizen.

### Strong phrasing

> Residue is lawful remainder relative to a chosen lens and proposed law.

That is compact and strong.

---

## 7. Admissible action

### Working definition

An **admissible action** is an action whose before-and-after image remains within the lawful family defined by the active lens.

This is the place where the current schema work begins to touch something more dynamic.

The existing binning schema does not yet define action in the full generative sense.
It does not yet say what moves are allowed by the underlying construction.
It does not yet decide admissibility by itself.

But it does provide the machinery by which admissibility can be tested.

That is because admissibility always needs some observational basis.

An action cannot be admissible in a vacuum.
It must be admissible **with respect to something**.

Examples of such conditions:

- closure score does not degrade beyond threshold
- residue remains bounded
- transport-role transition is allowed
- shell occupancy evolves within lawful range
- quotient compatibility is preserved
- parity or cocycle constraints are respected
- spectral instability is not introduced

So admissible action sits downstream of lens and image.

This gives a natural ladder:

carrier -> lens -> image -> law -> admissible action

And that ladder feels right.

### Why this is important

This means the present schema is not itself the action law,
but it may be the kernel of the apparatus that makes admissible action definable.

That is a very strong role.

## 8. Observer versus lens

It is worth writing this distinction explicitly.

### Observer

An observer, in the richer sense, may include:

- stance
- intention
- criteria
- memory
- root choice
- comparison strategy
- threshold choice
- update policy
- interpretation

### Lens

A lens is narrower and cleaner:

- it structures visibility
- it selects channels
- it organizes measurement
- it defines grouped appearance
- it constrains what can be seen in a disciplined way

So the current schema is not the observer.
It does not care.
It does not intend.
It does not interpret.
It does not choose aims.

But it does shape what can appear.

Hence:

> The schema is not the observer, but it is the observer apparatus in formalized form.

Or better still:

> The schema is the lens, not the seer.

That feels stable.

## 9. Why the JSON layer matters conceptually

It would be easy to dismiss the strict schema work as merely implementation detail.
That would be a mistake.

What the strict JSON layer does is formalize the geometry of observation.

It says:

- these are the lawful channels
- these are the allowed categories
- these are the required measurements
- these are the canonical distinctions
- these are the ways an image may be rendered stable enough for comparison

That is not just programming.
That is epistemic infrastructure.

If the G900 project becomes serious, then reproducible observation will matter.
If reproducible observation matters, then lenses must be formalized.
If lenses are formalized, then schema becomes a conceptual artifact, not only a technical one.

So the strict JSON layer is not merely support tooling.
It is part of the project’s observational mathematics.

## 10. Multiple lenses

A major strength of the lens language is that it naturally allows plurality.

There may not be one lens.
There may be a stack.

Examples:

### Shell lens
Makes radial occupancy and shell transitions visible.

### Growth lens
Makes birth ticks, epochs, and growth phases visible.

### Transport lens
Makes role classes, attachment types, and coupling behavior visible.

### Quotient lens
Makes effective descent structure and fiber behavior visible.

### Spectral lens
Makes mode participation, closure bands, and reduced operator behavior visible.

### Cocycle lens
Makes parity memory, sheet behavior, twist, and holonomy visible.

Each of these lenses may produce its own image.
Some laws may appear in one image only.
Some laws may persist across several lenses.
Some residues may disappear under one lens and intensify under another.

This is excellent.
It means the framework can grow without forcing premature flattening.

### Compound lens stack

The current schema work is likely not the kernel of a single lens only.
It may be the beginning of a **compound lens stack**.

That phrase feels promising.

---

## 11. Kernel

We should say carefully what “kernel” means here.

The current schema is not yet the whole lens because a whole lens may include:

- how the root is chosen
- how the carrier is normalized
- which quotient is active
- what thresholds define residue classes
- what comparisons are allowed
- what counts as lawful improvement
- how temporal updates are recorded
- what admissibility test is active

But the schema already defines the invariant frame into which such choices must later fit.

So “kernel” means:

- the minimal stable formal core
- the disciplined interior
- the part that can already be named without overclaiming

Hence:

> The strict schema is the kernel of the G900 lens.

That is probably the best current sentence.

---

## 12. What this is not

To keep the language clean, this note should also say what the lens is not.

The lens is not:

- the carrier
- the theorem
- the law itself
- the observer in the full sense
- the image
- the whole action calculus
- the quotient
- the spectrum
- the cocycle
- the final physical interpretation

It is also not just decoration, metaphor, or branding.
It has operational content.

The lens is the structured means by which lawful appearance becomes extractable.

That is enough.
It does not need inflation.

## 14. Immediate application to G900

Using the above language, the current project state can be described as follows.

### Carrier
G900, taken as a grown graph / transport object / emergent structured body.

### Lens kernel
The strict JSON-schema and binning framework now being defined.

### Image
Any resulting shell, transition, generation, role, quotient, or spectral summaries.

### Candidate laws
Any stable closure, overlap, descent, parity, or spectral relations discovered in those images.

### Residue
All measured deviation, anomaly, mismatch, or remainder relative to a candidate law.

### Admissible action
Future growth or transport moves that preserve or improve lawful image under the chosen lens.

That is already a coherent language game.
More importantly, it is a coherent mathematical posture.

## 15. A note on future development

The natural next development is not to abandon this lens language,
but to let it stratify.

A likely development sequence would be:

1. stabilize the lens kernel
2. produce sample images
3. identify persistent lawful relations
4. define residue classes
5. add action-transition logging
6. define admissibility criteria
7. compare multiple lenses on the same carrier
8. study which laws are lens-specific and which survive lens change

This is strong because it keeps the project honest.

It prevents the mistake of claiming a law before the image is stable.
It prevents the mistake of treating residue as noise too early.
It prevents the mistake of confusing a view with the whole object.

---

## 16. Closing orientation

The best sentence currently available may be this:

> The strict G900 schema is not the carrier and not yet the law. It is the kernel of the lens through which lawful image becomes extractable from the carrier.

That feels right because it preserves all the needed distinctions:

- the carrier is real
- the lens is structured
- the image is derived
- the law is discovered
- the residue remains informative
- admissible action becomes definable only after this observational stack is in place

That is enough for now.

The object need not yet tell us everything.
It has already told us how it wants to be seen.

And the name for that mode of seeing is lens.
