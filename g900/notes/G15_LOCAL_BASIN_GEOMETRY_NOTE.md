## 3. Subjective basin

### Scan summary
The subjective one-edit scan returned:

- `subjective-state`: 36
- `unresolved`: 12
- `action-cell`: 6
- `objective-state`: 0

Confidence summary:
- `nearest`: 42
- `ambiguous`: 12

### Interpretation
The subjective seed has a robust local subjective basin.

Most one-step perturbations remain nearest to the subjective family.

The basin does not leak directly into the objective family under the tested one-edit perturbations.

Instead, its non-subjective exits split into two kinds:

1. ambiguity along the subjective/objective boundary
2. spill into the action family

### Boundary mechanism
The ambiguity ridge appears at the hinge pair:

- changing `s2 -> s3`
- changing `t0 -> t3`

These are exactly the edits that mix the subjective and objective hinge coordinates.

Examples:
- `o0,o1,o2,s3,t0,s0`
- `o0,o1,o2,s2,t3,s0`

These classify as:
- `unresolved`
- `ambiguous`

with equal best distance to subjective and objective.

### Spillway mechanism
Edits that damage the outer `O` shell tend to push toward the action family rather than directly toward the objective family.

This suggests that:
- hinge damage induces state ambiguity
- shell/species damage induces action attraction

## 4. Objective basin

### Scan summary
The objective one-edit scan returned:

- `objective-state`: 36
- `unresolved`: 12
- `action-cell`: 6
- `subjective-state`: 0

Confidence summary:
- `nearest`: 42
- `ambiguous`: 12

### Interpretation
The objective seed has a robust local objective basin.

Its local behavior is strikingly parallel to the subjective basin.

Most one-step perturbations remain nearest to the objective family.

Like the subjective basin, it does not leak directly into the opposite state family under the tested one-edit perturbations.

Instead, its exits also split into:

1. ambiguity on the hinge ridge
2. spill into the action family

### Boundary mechanism
The objective hinge pair is:

- `s3`
- `t3`

The ambiguity ridge appears when these are shifted toward their subjective counterparts:

- `s3 -> s2`
- `t3 -> t0`

Examples:
- `o0,o1,o2,s2,t3,s0`
- `o0,o1,o2,s3,t0,s0`

Again these classify as:
- `unresolved`
- `ambiguous`

with equal best subjective/objective distance.

### Basin comparison with subjective
At present local resolution, the subjective and objective basins appear mirror-symmetric:

- same count profile
- same ambiguity count
- same action spill count
- no direct one-step leakage into each other

This is one of the strongest current structural observations.

## 5. Action basin

### Qualitative summary
The action basin is real, but it does not behave like a perfect third mirror of the two state basins.

It has a recognizable local action neighborhood:
- many one-edit perturbations remain nearest to `action-cell`
- many such perturbations have very small action distance, often 1

So action is not merely a residual category. It is a real attractor family.

### Important asymmetry
Unlike the state basins, the action basin appears to have preferred escape routes into the objective family.

From the current scan excerpts, several edits of the action seed classify as:
- `objective-state`
- `nearest`

Examples include edits such as:
- `edit_pos1_s2_to_s4`
- `edit_pos1_s2_to_t0`
- `edit_pos1_s2_to_t3`
- `edit_pos1_s2_to_t4`
- `edit_pos3_s0_to_o0`
- `edit_pos5_s3_to_s0`
- `edit_pos5_s3_to_o3`
- `edit_pos5_s3_to_o4`

This is the strongest current sign that the action basin is asymmetrically placed.

### Provisional interpretation
The action family appears to be:

- locally stable as its own species
- but more naturally adjacent to objective structure than to subjective structure

This suggests that action is not equally centered between subjective and objective.

Instead, it may be best understood as a third attractor with objective-leaning deformation channels.

## 6. Basin geometry picture

The current local geometric picture is:

- a subjective basin
- an objective basin
- an action basin
- a hinge ambiguity ridge separating the two state basins
- asymmetric action escape channels leaning toward objective

This is better pictured as:

subjective core <-> ambiguity ridge <-> objective core  
with action attached as a third chamber, but not symmetrically placed

A more suggestive phrasing is:

- subjective and objective are the two primary state chambers
- action is a genuine third chamber
- action appears locally more compatible with objective closure than with subjective return

---

## 7. Current best structural distinction

The classifier results now support two distinct local failure modes.

### A. Hinge failure
Perturbing the interior hinge pair produces state ambiguity.

This is the subjective/objective boundary phenomenon.

### B. Species-shell failure
Perturbing the outer shell grammar produces action attraction.

This is the state/action separation phenomenon.

So the machine is not merely classifying labels. It is detecting two different geometric mechanisms.

This is currently the most interesting asymmetry in the local geometry.
